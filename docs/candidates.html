Google Search Trends: 2020 Democratic Presidential Candidates

The 2020 Presidential election is still more than a year away, yet politicians have already begun campaigning and debating. Google search trends reveal how much interest the nation has in three front-runners: Joe Biden, Bernie Sanders, and Elizabeth Warren.

Humera Lodhi

Published October 2019

Who gets Googled the most?

Since the first presidential debate, Bernie Sanders has largely been the most Googled candidates-- both in the United States and globaly. There are a few notable incidents when Joe Biden or Elizabeth Warren recieved a surge of interest, like after the first Democratic debate.

News coverage trends, however, tell a different story. The number of stories about Joe Biden far exceed the number of stories about Bernie Sanders or Elizabeth Warren.

What does each state think of the candidates?

States have varying levels of interest in the candidates. Each candidate recieves more interest than the other two in their home states. Elizabeth Warren recieves the most interest from the three candidates in the District of Columbia, while most of the states are split between Bernie Sanders and Joe Biden in terms of most searched candidate in the area.
